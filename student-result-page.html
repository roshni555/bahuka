Tips to Know

Auditory

  • Use Your Phone to record notes
  • Read your notes aloud when Studying.
  • Work with a study partner to review out loud.
  • Work in a group where you can discuss the information.
  • Record lectures and note times in lecture you aren't understanding.
  • Review these recorded times later for extra review.
  • When learning new material,especially equations,talk your way through the material.
  • Use of metaphors similes to compare and remember(say them out loud).
  • Use online resources apps like Youtube, Vimeo, and TED Talks.

    • Visual

  • Use the margins to draw diagrams of your notes,or your notes app.
  • Create a flowchart for the progression of your notes and ideas.
  • Make flashcards with visual clues.
  • Highlight key information in your texts or notes.
  • Create a chart or a series of different color boxes to write information in, use colors to remember information.
  • Use your laptops to convert data and notes into charts, table, graphics, pictures, etc.

    • Kinesthetic

  • Assemble charts and diagrams.
  • Take breaks when studying.
  • Combine an activity when studying.
  • Use tools such as flashcards and mnemonics.
  • Keep your fingers busy while studying.

    • Textual

  • Re-write your notes after class.
  • Highlight important information.
  • Write notes to yourself on the side.
  • Write out key concepts and ideas to focus on.
  • Compose short explanations for diagrams,charts,and graps.
  • Write out instructions for each steps of a procedure or math problem.
  • Use post-its in visible places to review (like by the mirror when you are brushing your teeth).
